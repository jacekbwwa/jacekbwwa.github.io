WELCOME TO TATRA TRAVEL

Company specializing in Climbing Hiking Adventures Expeditions

Alps-like Tatra Mountains, or the Tatras (Polish Tatry), is the highest range of the Carpathian Mountains.
Two hour drive from Krakow, one can find stunning views and pristine nature. Our buses take you straight from Krakow City Centre to Zakopane, which offers various activities in nearby mountains throughout the year, like skiing and hiking, rock climbing, cave exploring, cycling, paragliding, etc. Tourism has been our main business for over a decade. Tourists can expect meals in a bus and are thoroughly catered for. Annually, 20,000 of them turn up in the Polish alps through Tatra Travel.
Let us know about your travel expectations, the time you wish to travel, we will provide you the best programs and perfect holiday vacations.

SUMMER ➔

There is 275 km of marked tourist trails located in the Tatra National Park (the park was established in 1954 and covers an area of 21,197 hectares), with various degrees of difficulty from very easy to extremely difficult ones, fitted with safety equipment, such as chains, iron steps or rope ladders. The various colours (yellow, red, blue, green and black) the trails are marked with, do not indicate the degree of difficulty, but they help to identify the individual route in the open area. Our explorers also get along well with wildlife preservation which is paramount seeing that twin National parks cover the whole of the Tatras both sides of the national border. Click above if you want to book your Summer Experience Package! We offer happy adventures travel trekking mountaineering services.

WINTER ➔

During this time, you will find alpine terrain covered with snow, ice, rock and frozen grass. Lots of routes in Poland are quite difficult during the summer seasons due to presence of grass and loose rocks, but during the winter the grass patches freeze over and you can stick your ice tool in it, like in ice. Getting scared? Do not be frozen, that kind grass is mainly found on easy pitches. The harder pitches are usually rock. If climbing conditions are good, then you have consolidated snow, frozen grass, lots of alpine ice and sub zero temperatures. Click above if you want to book your Winter Experience Package!

MAP ➔

To get there you can fly to Kraków, and from there you have about 110 km to get to Zakopane. There is plenty of buses to choose from, departing from Krakow main bus station. Once you are there, the local transport companies can take you to the surrounding valleys (Morskie Oko, Hala Gasienicowa) or to Slovakia. With easy communication from Kraków to Zakopane, it is entirely possible to organise a long climbing weekend in the Tatras. Having trouble getting there? Our maps will show you the way.
